Week Four Cultural Blog

Discuss a time you had an emotional conflict with someone. What was it about? What happened?

I was bartending, talking with a customer about their order and a server interrupts us about a ticket. He did this quite often and at this point it was just plain rude and disrespectful to the customer. I tell him that he needs to stop interrupting like that because it is disrespectful to the customer and to be a little more patient. He snapped back at me with a rude comment about me taking forever and some nasty words following.

What was the source of the conflict?

I believe he was stressed out so instead of acting appropriately he lashed out which resulted in me lashing out.

How did you handle that conflict?

I told him that if he acts that way, I will just take longer to get him his drinks. I did so in some sense because what he said was very irritating and rude.

Did your actions make the conflict better or worse?

My actions weren't mature or appropriate, but he stopped interrupting and being impatient so it benefited me.

If you could go back, what would you do differently, if anything?

I mean, what I did wasn't exactly appropriate, but his response to me politely saying that it is disrespectful to interrupt the customer and needs to be more patient was completely inappropriate and unnecessary. I guess I would have maybe not purposely took forever with the drinks, but because I did he stopped his negative actions.

What did you learn from this experience?

Stress makes people do things they normally wouldn't if calm. Also that I desperately need to get out of the restaurant business.
