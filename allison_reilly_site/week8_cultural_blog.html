Week Eight Cultural Blog

What was it like to pair with others to solve challenges? Was something particularly fun or rewarding? Was something frustrating and difficult for you?

At first, I didn't like it. That was because we were working on HTML and CSS and I feel confident, maybe too confident, in my abilities. It made it difficult to work with others since I didn't feel like it was benefiting me. Once I started on other types of challenges, like Ruby and Javascript, working with others was great. I have a decent understanding for the basics, but it is a learning experience working with others and helps broaden your way of thinking out the problem. Plus, I got to work with people who are intelligent with similar interests as me which is very enjoyable.


Feedback

How did you feel when you read your feedback?

Well, my first few reviews made me feel upset with myself. I tend to be controlling and will take over if I feel confident in the challenge. I a couple pieces of feedback where people basically stated that in a kind way. I have been working on it though, and the rest of my feedback has been very positive.

Was the feedback you received helpful to your learning?

Yes, it helps me know what I need to work on and what I am doing right. If the feedback is positive, it makes me feel good.

Based on the feedback you've received, what are you going to do to improve next time you pair?

Well, my feedback has been pretty positive, I have been working with others sharing roles of driving and navigating. I think all I need to do is continue to remember to work equally with others and not take over.

How was it to write feedback?

It was good, I hope I was specific enough. Mostly everyone I have worked with has been great so there is nothing negative to state.

What was most difficult for you when writing feedback for your pair?

The first feedback I had to write, I felt like the person didn't work well with me and didn't put any input, even when I asked for it. It was tough figuring out how to write how I felt without sounding harsh.

Overall, what do you think of using pairing and feedback to guide your learning?

I think its great. I enjoy pairing, it is a great learning experience. I need to work on writing feedback more detailed, but I have found the feedback I have received to be very helpful.
